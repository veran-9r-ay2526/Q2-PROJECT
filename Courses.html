Designs of the Classrooms

Come and discover how our classrooms, artistically enhanced by the design team of NAZARENO / LICHAUCO, support the philosophy and educational method of Dr. Maria Montessori. Each classroom has materials and spaces that encourage independent learning, collaboration, creativity, and hands-on activities to foster an environment where students can thrive.

. .

Casa Classrooms

Basic forms and colors are utilized to give the essence of being in nature—with trees, the ground, and the sun—like being ‘cocooned’ in a park.

. .

Primary Classrooms

Advancing to more complex forms, the Primary Classrooms inhabit a conceptual biosphere—with water, earth, and air represented on the walls, including a burst of sunshine by the mirror.

. .

Intermediate Classrooms

Science and inventions by both men and women throughout history—from Leonardo da Vinci, Nikola Tesla to Letitia Mumford Geer—highlight the Intermediate Classrooms.

. .

Junior Highschool Classrooms

Silhouettes of important work from all over the world, including the Philippines, in design-based disciplines such as furniture/visual/industrial design, fashion, and architecture are featured in the Junior High Classrooms. Images such as Zaha Hadid architecture, the Philippine jeepney, and Charles & Ray Eames’ furniture are among those presented.

. .

Senior Highschool Classrooms

Each of the three classrooms celebrates an important figure in Philippine Contemporary art—Roberto Chabet, Nina Saguil, and Leo Valledor.
